Dear Sir

I write to express my distress and anger at the way I was treated on my arrival at Heathrow airport last weekend with my 6-year-old daughter. Shortly after passing through passport control, we were approached by two Border Force officers who asked us to follow them. I was separated from my daughter who had to wait in another room. I was taken to a room with a table and chair, no ventilation, no window, with no access to water. They took my phone, locked the door and left. I was left for 3 hours like this and despite shouting and banging at the door no one came to help me or to explain why I was being imprisoned.

After over 3 hours one of the officers returned and asked for my passport. This was upsetting and I asked to be allowed to see my daughter and ring my husband. I was told that ‘it was not appropriate for me to see her or to call anyone at this time’. I then told them I suffered from claustrophobia and couldn’t be left alone in such a small room but was openly mocked for being immature. I asked for food and water and was told that ‘this wasn’t a café'.

I said I had a headache and wanted a paracetamol and was told that someone would bring me something. This didn’t happen.

A further 3 hours passed before anyone returned. This time, the two male officers were accompanied by a female who ordered me to remove my clothes. To my horror, I was subjected to what I can only describe the most humiliating and intrusive of searches by this woman. The two male officers remained in the room while this was happening. I still feel sick with humiliation.

After a total of nearly 8 hours, I was allowed to leave and see my daughter. She was nearly as upset as I was. I got no explanation, no apology and no help. I do not understand why I was subjected to this inhuman and degrading treatment.

I have been traumatised by this experience. I demand an explanation. I have been a British citizen all my life.

Yours sincerely

Jane Smith
